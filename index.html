About

Dr. Mohamed Mohsen is a marine ecologist with a Ph.D. in Marine Ecology from the Chinese Academy of Sciences, China. His research focuses on the environmental impacts of microplastics and nanoplastics, aquatic animal health, aquaculture ecology, and the resilience of aquatic species to pollutants and environmental stressors.

He is currently an Assistant Professor at the Faculty of Agriculture, Al-Azhar University. He has also worked as a postdoctoral scholar at Jimei University and the Institute of Oceanology, Chinese Academy of Sciences, where he investigated microplastic contamination in aquafeed, sea cucumber products, aquaculture environments, and aquatic organisms.

Research Interests

Microplastics in Aquaculture

Investigating sources, abundance, composition, and ecological risks of microplastics in aquaculture environments, aquafeed, and aquatic organisms.

Sea Cucumber Biology

Studying ingestion, transfer, accumulation, and biological effects of microplastics and other stressors in sea cucumbers.

Ecotoxicology

Evaluating oxidative stress, tissue pathology, physiological responses, and toxicological impacts of microplastics, nanoplastics, heavy metals, and climate-related stressors.

Aquatic Animal Health

Assessing how environmental pollutants and aquaculture conditions affect fish, invertebrates, and other aquatic species.
