About

Dr. Mohamed Mohsen is a marine ecologist with research experience in aquatic pollution, microplastics, nanoplastics, aquaculture, sea cucumber biology, and ecotoxicology. His work examines how emerging contaminants and environmental stressors affect aquatic organisms, aquaculture environments, seafood safety, and marine ecosystem health.

His research includes studies on microplastic ingestion, transfer, accumulation, and toxicity in sea cucumbers; microplastic contamination in aquafeed and seafood; heavy metals associated with plastics; and biological responses of aquatic animals to pollutants and climate-related stressors.

Research Focus

Microplastics in Aquaculture

Investigating microplastic sources, abundance, polymer composition, and ecological risks in aquaculture systems, aquafeed, and cultured organisms.

Sea Cucumber Ecology

Studying microplastic ingestion, transfer, tissue accumulation, and physiological responses in sea cucumbers, especially Apostichopus japonicus.

Ecotoxicology

Assessing oxidative stress, tissue pathology, inflammation, development, survival, and physiological disruption caused by microplastics, nanoplastics, heavy metals, and combined stressors.

Environmental Health

Evaluating the links between plastic pollution, seafood contamination, aquaculture sustainability, and aquatic animal health.
